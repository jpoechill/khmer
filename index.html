About the Khmer Language

Khmer is the official language of Cambodia and is spoken by over 16 million people. It has a rich literary tradition and unique script, with deep cultural and historical significance.

Schools & University Programs

Several institutions in the United States and abroad offer Khmer language instruction:

  • University of Hawaii at Manoa – BA in Southeast Asian Studies with Khmer courses
  • California State University, Long Beach – Khmer language courses and Southeast Asian Studies
  • University of Washington – Khmer courses through Southeast Asian Languages and Cultures
  • Northern Illinois University – Offers Khmer through the Center for Southeast Asian Studies
  • Laney College (Oakland, CA) – Community Khmer language classes
  • American University of Phnom Penh – Undergraduate degrees incorporating Khmer

Books for Learning the Khmer Language

  • Introduction to Khmer by David D. Smyth – A clear and accessible guide for beginners in Khmer script and conversation.
  • Complete Khmer by Richard K. Gilbert – A comprehensive self-study course with audio support.
  • Khmer for Foreigners by Southeast Asia Language School – A structured workbook used in many Southeast Asian programs.
  • Khmer Reader by Franklin Huffman – Intermediate texts to build vocabulary and comprehension in cultural context.
  • Spoken Cambodian by Robert B. Jones – A classic course with phonetics and useful dialogues.

Books on Modern Culture and History

  • Hun Sen’s Cambodia by Sebastian Strangio – A deep dive into Cambodia’s modern political landscape under Prime Minister Hun Sen.
  • Voices from S-21 by David Chandler – An analysis of the Khmer Rouge’s notorious Tuol Sleng prison, exploring history and memory.
  • Cambodia’s Curse by Joel Brinkley – Investigates the nation's post-Khmer Rouge challenges and ongoing political issues.
  • Forest Guardians, Forest Destroyers by Tim K. G. Baird – Examines environmental struggles in Cambodia, including deforestation and land rights.
  • Performing the Nation by J. C. Thomas – Cultural expression and national identity in contemporary Cambodia.
